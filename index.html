Fun Facts about Camels:

  1. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

Fun Facts about Chameleons:

  1. They change body color to communicate or regulate their body temperature.
  2. They don't regrow thier tails like lizards .
  3. Their feet work like salad tongs.

Fun Facts about Cicadas:

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall.
  2. Most have red-orange eyes.
  3. Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.

Fun Facts about Pandas:

  1. They spend a lot of their day eating.
  2. They have great camouflage for their environment.
  3. Their eyes are different than normal bears.

Fun Facts about Pigeons:

  1. The pigeon can recognise all 26 letters of the English language as well as being able to conceptualise.
  2. Adult pigeons do occasionally kill their babies.
  3. Pigeons are highly sociable animals.

Fun Facts about Teddy Bears:

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall!
  3. The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Fun Facts about Frogs:

  1. There is evidence that frogs have roamed the Earth for more than 200 million years.
  2. One of the smallest is the Cuban tree toad, which grows to half an inch long.
  3. The world's largest frog is the goliath frog of West Africa—it can grow to 15 inches and weigh up to 7 pounds.

Fun Facts about Dogs:

  1. Their sense of smell is at least 40x better than ours.
  2. Dogs don't sweat like we do.
  3. Some dogs are incredible swimmers.
